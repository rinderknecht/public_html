Syllabus of the Networking course

Goals

The goal of this course is to give an overview of computer networks, following a top-down approach and featuring the Internet. ``Top-down'' means that we will always start with concepts that are the most accessible to your intuition and experience (of the Internet), and then dig the technicalities. Typically, we will not start with the details of a network card or signal processing, but instead try to give a feeling of what networking is by providing as much context as possible. We will describe more than we define precisely. Details come come in a second step.

How to achieve the goals

The complete slide presentation is already available at the author's web site, so students should read some slides in advance before attending the corresponding class. This way, they also can get a clear meaning of the language --- since this course is taught in English. Exercises will be given during class in order to assess student's understanding and for training purposes. Two quizzes will be formally given too. Each time, the answers will be available from the author's web site in a short delay.

Textbook

This course is based on the book by James F. Kurose and Keith W. Ross, entitled Computer Networks: A Top-Down Approach Featuring the Internet, published by Pearson and Addison Wesley, in 2003, third edition. Note that the third edition is translated in Korean and a fourth edition is available in English.
