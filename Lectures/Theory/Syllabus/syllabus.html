Syllabus of the course on a Simple Theory of Computation

Summary

The goal of this course is to introduce the student to the concepts of programming languages. He is supposed to already have a practical understanding of programming but he usually does not reflect on the concepts involved in the design and programming activities, focusing too much on hitting the keyboard. This course therefore revisits the basics of computing, algorithms and data structures, allowing the student to more easily assess new programming languages by relating them to what he already knows, based on the concepts they involved, and not on the vendors' marketing.

Prerequisites

None.

Textbook

This course does not require any book in particular, as the focus is mainly in doing lots of short homeworks.

Evaluation

Two examinations as usual, plus homeworks twice a week. Exercises will be solved during class-time and answers posted on the web site. Attendance will be checked and taken into account for the final grade.
