Syllabus of the Compiler Construction course

Summary

The aim of this course is to introduce the students to the arcanes of compiler construction. At the end of the course, the students should be able to understand the concepts involved in compiler construction and, in particular, understand in details the first stage of compilation, called lexical analysis. This theoritical part of the course is very important because compilers are excellent examples of complex software systems which only can be implemented by combining theory and practice. Consequently, students also should be able to implement lexical analysers using the tool Lex.

Prerequisites

None.

Textbook

This course does not require any book in particular, as the focus is mainly in doing lots of short homeworks.

Evaluation

Two examinations as usual, plus homeworks twice a week. Exercises will be solved during class-time and answers posted on the web site. Attendance will be checked and taken into account for the final grade.
